Artificial Intelligence

Artificial intelligence (AI) is intelligence demonstrated by machines, as opposed to natural intelligence
displayed by animals including humans. AI research has been defined as the field of study of intelligent
agents, which refers to any system that perceives its environment and takes actions that maximize its
chance of achieving its goals.

The term "artificial intelligence" had previously been used to describe machines that mimic and display
"human" cognitive skills associated with the human mind, such as "learning" and "problem-solving".
This definition has since been rejected by major AI researchers who now describe AI in terms of
rationality and acting rationally, which does not limit how intelligence can be articulated.

AI applications include advanced web search engines such as Google Search, recommendation systems
used by YouTube, Amazon, and Netflix, understanding human speech such as Siri and Alexa,
self-driving cars such as Waymo, generative AI tools such as ChatGPT, and competing at the
highest level in strategic games such as chess and Go.

History of Artificial Intelligence

The field of AI research was founded at a workshop held on the campus of Dartmouth College during
the summer of 1956. Those who attended would become the leaders of AI research for decades. Many
of them predicted that a machine as intelligent as a human being would exist in no more than a
generation, and they were given millions of dollars to make this vision come true. Eventually it
became obvious that commercial developers and some government contractors had taken a much more
pragmatic approach and that there were major hurdles in the way.

The first AI winter (1974–1980): By 1974, the progress in AI had slowed significantly. The AI
community had been unable to deliver on its promises, and government funding was cut sharply.
Researchers such as Minsky and Papert had published Perceptrons (1969), which showed that
two-layer neural networks could solve only linearly separable problems.

The expert systems era (1980s): AI research was revived in the 1980s with the rise of expert
systems. An expert system is a program that answers questions or solves problems in a specific
domain of knowledge, using logical rules derived from the knowledge of experts.

Deep Learning Revolution (2010s): The field was transformed by the success of deep learning,
a subset of machine learning. Deep learning uses artificial neural networks with many layers
to learn representations of data. The success of AlexNet in the 2012 ImageNet competition
demonstrated that deep convolutional neural networks could achieve state-of-the-art performance.

Machine Learning Approaches

Machine learning is a subfield of AI. It is a method of data analysis that automates analytical
model building. It is based on the idea that systems can learn from data, identify patterns and
make decisions with minimal human intervention. There are several types of machine learning:

Supervised learning is the machine learning task of learning a function that maps an input to
an output based on example input-output pairs. It infers a function from labeled training data
consisting of a set of training examples.

Unsupervised learning is a type of algorithm that learns patterns from untagged data. The hope
is that through mimicry, which is computationally cheaper than other approaches, the machine
is forced to build a compact internal representation of its world.

Reinforcement learning (RL) is an area of machine learning concerned with how intelligent
agents ought to take actions in an environment in order to maximize the notion of cumulative
reward. The agent learns to achieve a goal in an uncertain, potentially complex environment.

Neural Networks and Deep Learning

An artificial neural network (ANN) is modeled loosely after the human brain that is designed to
recognize patterns. They interpret sensory data through a kind of machine perception, labeling
or clustering raw input. The patterns they recognize are numerical, contained in vectors, into
which all real-world data, be it images, sound, text or time series, must be translated.

Deep learning is part of a broader family of machine learning methods based on artificial neural
networks with representation learning. Learning can be supervised, semi-supervised or unsupervised.

Convolutional neural networks (CNN) are specialized types of artificial neural networks designed
to process pixel data and are used in image recognition and processing. They use a mathematical
operation called convolution that scans input data and applies a filter to produce a feature map.

Recurrent neural networks (RNN) are a type of artificial neural networks designed to recognize
sequences and patterns in data. Unlike feedforward networks, RNNs have connections that form
cycles, allowing output from some nodes to affect subsequent input to the same nodes.

The Turing Test

The Turing test is a test of a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behaviour equivalent to,
or indistinguishable from, that of a human. Alan Turing proposed it in 1950, in his paper
"Computing Machinery and Intelligence". The test involves a human judge engaging in natural
language conversations with both a human and a machine, with the judge trying to determine
which is which.

Applications of AI

AI has been used in healthcare for disease diagnosis, drug discovery, and personalized medicine.
Computer vision systems can detect cancer from medical images with superhuman accuracy.
Natural language processing enables AI to understand and generate human text.
Autonomous vehicles use AI for perception, decision-making, and control.
Robotics AI enables machines to perform physical tasks in unstructured environments.

Ethical Considerations

As AI systems become more capable, questions about safety, bias, fairness, privacy, and the
long-term future of humanity have become increasingly important. Bias in AI systems can
perpetuate and amplify existing societal inequalities. Privacy concerns arise when AI systems
process large amounts of personal data. Questions about accountability and transparency are
raised when AI systems make consequential decisions.
